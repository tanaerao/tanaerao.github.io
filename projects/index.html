GiveWell Change Our Mind Contest Submissions

August - November 2022

I co-authored two submissions to GiveWell’s Change Our Mind contest, both of which received honourable mentions ($2500 cash prize).

The first submission, co-authored with Sam Nolan and Hannah Rokebrand, refactored the entire GiveWell cost effectiveness analysis to quantify the uncertainty in each parameter, then argued that such uncertainty quantification is decision-relevant. I only did about 5-10% of the work for this project. I also received a prize individually for a previous draft of my section (on the Against Malaria Foundation) from the developers of Squiggle, a “simple programming language for intuitive probabilistic estimation” ($600).

The second submission, co-authored with Ricky Huang, argued that GiveWell’s current approach to weighing different moral outcomes is flawed, and suggested that GiveWell hire an academic practical ethicist (perhaps on a part-time or project basis) to conduct a thorough review of the current system.

Scoring in British Parliamentary Debating

2021-ongoing

The way that international university debating tournaments are currently scored has some surprising implications on accuracy and fairness. As an active university debater, I’m interested in using both theoretical models and data from previous tournaments to better understand these scoring systems. Like most people who have been curious about these issues over the past few years, I only work on this occasionally.
